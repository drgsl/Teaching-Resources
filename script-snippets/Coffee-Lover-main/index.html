History

The original domesticated coffee plant is said to have been from Harar, and the native population is thought to be derived from Ethiopia with distinct nearby populations in Sudan and Kenya. Coffee was primarily consumed in the Islamic world where it originated and was directly related to religious practices. The word "coffee" entered the English language in 1582 via the Dutch koffie, borrowed from the Ottoman Turkish kahve, in turn borrowed from the Arabic qahwah.

Types

Different Types of Coffee Beans

There are four different types of coffee beans: Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa. The most common (and popular) are Arabica and Robusta, but you might get lucky and run into the other two.

Arabica tends to have a smoother, sweeter taste, with flavour notes of chocolate and sugar. They often also have hints of fruits or berries. Robusta, on the other hand, has a stronger, harsher and more bitter taste, with grainy or rubbery overtones.

The Espresso

The length of one espresso shot can be ristretto (or stretto) (reduced), normale or standard (normal), or lungo (long).
Proportions will vary and the volume of the crema will also be different depending on the measurements used.
For example, a ristretto is usually half of a volume of normale and lungo is double the normale volume.

The Doppio

A Doppio is a double espresso in a cup. No other ingredient is added to it.
To create a double espresso all you need is two shots of espresso.

The Americano

To make an Americano, you must pour the hot water first.
Fill two-thirds of a cup with hot water and add one shot of espresso over the hot water.

The Cappuccino

A cappuccino is made by pouring an espresso shot into a cup, then top it with steamed milk and foam in an equal ration.
You should sprinkle ground chocolate on top of the foam, if you like it more fancy.

The Latte

To create the perfect latte extract one shot of espresso, then pour steamed milk over.
The latte should have a minimal amount of milk foam on top, used to create "latte art".

The Mocha

A mocha is a cross between a cappuccino and a hot chocolate.
The idea behind a mocha is to have an extraordinarily extravagant drink.
A shot of espresso, a small part of hot chocolate (or hot chocolate syrup), topped with steamed milk and milk foam.

The Macchiato

The macchiato is another espresso-based drink that has a small amount of foam on top.
It’s the happy medium between a cappuccino and a doppio. Just note that different baristas will have their own way of doing the macchiato.
You will find some who add a dollop of steamed milk and a dollop of milk foam.

The Irish Coffee

It's a delightful and cheerful and basically classed more of a cocktail than a coffee beverage.
Irish coffee is dunk through the cream.
The traditional way is as follows: 8 cl of espresso, 4 cl of whiskey, 3 cl fresh cream and 1 tsp of brown sugar.
Sit back, relax and sip a delicious combo of espresso and whiskey through a thick layer of indulgent whipped cream.
